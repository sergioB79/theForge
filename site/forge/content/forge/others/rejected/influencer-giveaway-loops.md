SUBTITLE: Bright Circles, Hollow Centers

Influencer giveaway loops belong to the age of quantified attention: intricate, optimized, and not without a certain brutal elegance. But the Forge is for structures that bind presence to consequence, and here the stakes dissolve the moment the metric is captured. What looks like collective ritual is, on contact, revealed as a choreography of incentives with no shared center of gravity.

‚ùå Why It Fails the Forge:
- **Structural failure**  
  The loop is an apparatus for circulation, not transformation. It demands clicks, not presence; compliance, not encounter. Participants are interchangeable units in a growth equation, and nothing in the design requires them to be *there* in any meaningful sense. When the loop ends, no new structure of relation or perception is required to remain.

- **Ethical failure**  
  The primary vector is instrumentalization: people are treated as leverage, their attention collateralized for reach. Hope is induced, then mathematically betrayed for most entrants by design. Consequences are asymmetrical and often invisible: quiet erosion of trust, worsening comparison, the normalization of extracting social graphs under the guise of generosity.

- **Symbolic failure**
