SUBTITLE: Sheltering Hands That Twist the World

INFO: Moral hazard names the distortion that occurs when people are shielded from the full consequences of their actions. It lives at the junction of insurance, finance, politics, and private life. Wherever protection grows, temptation to reckless use grows with it.

This is not an elegant criticism of bankers; it is a bleak mirror for anyone who has ever been covered, backed, or forgiven‚Äîand then felt their caution thin. Moral hazard is the quiet law that risk, once softened, will try to expand until it fills all available shelter.

Moral hazard survives silence because it keeps reappearing in places we want to believe are exempt: families, friendships, welfare, markets, war. It resists simplification by binding together trust, power, and self-deception: no one involved is purely villain or victim. It reconstructs reality by forcing us to redesign systems with the expectation that protection will be abused, not just hoped against. The longer you hold it, the heavier it gets, because you begin to see how often your mercy, your safety nets, your guarantees are not neutral kindnesses but changes to the moral physics of the room.
