Do not come to Arendt looking for a moral mascot or a definitive verdict on history. Read her as you would listen to someone who has seen the center of the world collapse and is still arguing, in real time, about what words might hold reality together. Allow yourself to be offended and then ask why. Keep her sentences in tension with the lived pain they attempt to categorize; hold both her courage and her coldness in view. Let her challenge your desire to outsource judgment to systems, ideologies, or historical inevitability. Above all, do not adopt her conclusions until you have first subjected them to the same scrutiny she demanded of herself.
